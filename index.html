What is BridgeConnect?

BridgeConnect is a modern platform that housing providers use to coordinate mutual exchanges on behalf of their tenants. When a resident needs to move closer to family, work, or specialist services, their housing team records the requirements and BridgeConnect handles the rest.

Smart Matching

Our system automatically finds compatible properties based on your needs

Mutual Exchanges

Enables direct swaps between tenants for faster moves

Faster Process

Automated workflows reduce waiting time significantly

Transparent

Track your transfer status every step of the way

How It Works in Simple Terms

When you ask to move, your housing officer records your needs inside BridgeConnect. The platform searches for available properties or other tenants who could swap. Housing providers on both sides review and approve the proposal, coordinate dates, and keep you updated throughout.

Tenant Access

Tenants do not log into BridgeConnect directly. Every action inside the system is completed by housing providers, who capture information from conversations with residents and share updates back with them.

Real Example

Sarah lives in Leeds but works in Manchester. She needs a 2-bedroom home closer to work.

John lives in Manchester but his family is in Leeds. He also needs a 2-bedroom home.

BridgeConnect notifies both housing providers, the officers arrange the approvals and logistics, and the swap happens in weeks instead of months.

The Transfer Process

The mutual exchange process follows a clear path from your initial request to moving day. Here's what happens at each stage:

1

Registration & Profile

Initial Setup

Your housing officer creates your profile with basic information about you and your family. This includes household size, any medical needs, and your current property details.

  • Personal and family information recorded
  • Current living situation documented
  • Medical needs noted (if applicable)
  • GDPR consent obtained for data sharing
2

Transfer Request (Nomination)

Seeking Match

Your housing officer captures the transfer request (Nomination) in BridgeConnect, detailing the areas you want to live in, the size of home required, and the reasons for moving.

  • Housing provider records preferred locations (e.g., "Needs to move to Leeds")
  • Bedroom and property requirements documented
  • Reason for transfer captured (work, family, medical, etc.)
  • Any special considerations noted
Snapshot Taken

When your housing provider submits the request, BridgeConnect stores a snapshot of your circumstances. If something changes, the officer can submit an updated request.

3

Matching

Match Found

Our system automatically searches for compatible matches. This could be a vacant property that meets your needs, or another tenant who wants to swap.

  • System runs daily to find new matches
  • Matches scored based on compatibility
  • Both housing associations notified of potential match
  • Officers review the match details
4

Review & Approval

Approved

Housing officers from both associations review the match. They check that it makes sense for everyone involved and approve the transfer.

  • Your housing association approves you leaving
  • Receiving housing association approves you moving in
  • If it's a swap, both sides must approve both tenants
  • All approvals must be in place before proceeding
5

Moving Day Preparation

Scheduled

Once everything is approved, a move date is scheduled. You'll be given time to prepare and arrange your moving logistics.

  • Move date agreed with all parties
  • Keys handover arranged
  • Property inspections scheduled
  • Moving arrangements made
6

Move Complete

Completed

You move to your new home! The system records when you leave your old property and when you move into your new one.

  • Keys to old property returned
  • Keys to new property received
  • Transfer marked as complete
  • Welcome to your new home!
Success!

Your transfer is complete. You're now settled in your new home and registered with your new housing association.

Simple Transfer

A simple transfer happens when you move to a vacant property. This is the most straightforward type of transfer - no swaps involved!

Example Scenario

Tenant: Emma Wilson

Current: 1-bedroom flat in Liverpool

Needs: 2-bedroom ground floor property in Manchester

Reason: New baby arriving, wants to be closer to family

Available Property

Address: 45 Oak Lane, Manchester

Type: 2-bedroom ground floor flat

Status: Vacant and ready

The Journey

Step 1

Transfer Request (Nomination) Submitted

Emma explains her needs to her housing officer, who enters the transfer request (Nomination) into BridgeConnect with family details, medical information, and GDPR consent.

Step 2

Match Found

The system finds 45 Oak Lane - a perfect match! It's in Manchester (where Emma wants to be), has 2 bedrooms (what she needs), and it's ground floor (important for the baby).

Step 3

Approvals

Liverpool HA approves Emma leaving. Manchester HA reviews her application and approves her moving in. Both sides are happy with the match.

Step 4

Property Viewing & Move Scheduled

Emma views the property and loves it! Move date is set for 2 weeks' time. She starts packing and arranging her move.

Step 6

Moving Day

Emma moves out of her Liverpool flat and into her new Manchester home. Keys are exchanged, and the transfer is complete!

Result

Emma is now settled in Manchester, closer to her family, with enough space for her growing family. Total time: 6 weeks!

2-Way Swap (Mutual Exchange)

A 2-way swap is when two housing providers coordinate a direct exchange between tenants whose needs perfectly align. Officers handle the data entry, approvals, and scheduling so residents can focus on preparing for their moves.

Example Scenario

Tenant A: James Brown (Manchester)

Current: 3-bedroom house in Manchester city center

Wants: 2-bedroom property in Leeds (closer to work)

Reason: New job in Leeds, children grown up and moved out

Tenant B: Lisa Chen (Leeds)

Current: 2-bedroom flat in Leeds

Wants: 3-bedroom house in Manchester (growing family)

Reason: Expecting second child, wants bigger space near family

Perfect Match!

James needs Lisa's 2-bed in Leeds, and Lisa needs James's 3-bed in Manchester. Their housing providers can link the two requests and create a direct swap so everyone gets what they need.

The Journey

Step 1

Requests Logged by Housing Teams

James' housing officer records his need for a 2-bed in Leeds. Lisa's officer records her need for a 3-bed in Manchester. Both requests now sit in the BridgeConnect matching pool.

Step 2

System Detects a Direct Swap

The matching system runs and sees that James needs Lisa's location and Lisa needs James'. Housing officers receive the alert and review the details.

Step 3

Exchange Created & Approvals

The system creates a 2-way exchange linking both transfers. Manchester HA approves both tenants. Leeds HA approves both tenants. All four approvals are in place!

Step 4

Move Date Coordinated

The housing providers coordinate a single moving day with both tenants. In a swap, everyone must move on the same day (or very close together) so properties don't sit vacant.

Step 5

Swap Day!

James moves to Leeds. Lisa moves to Manchester. Keys are exchanged under the supervision of both housing teams. Both transfers complete simultaneously. Everyone's happy!

Coordination is Key

In a 2-way swap, both moves must happen together. If one person backs out, the entire exchange is cancelled. This protects everyone involved.

Result

James is closer to his new job in Leeds. Lisa has the space her growing family needs in Manchester. Both got exactly what they wanted! Total time: 5 weeks.

3-Way Chain

A 3-way chain happens when three housing providers coordinate moves in a circle - each tenant needs the area currently occupied by another. It is more complex, but it unlocks moves that wouldn't be possible with vacant stock alone.

Example Scenario

Tenant A: David (Manchester)

Current: 2-bedroom flat in Manchester

Wants: 2-bedroom in Leeds

Reason: Closer to aging parents

Tenant B: Sarah (Leeds)

Current: 2-bedroom house in Leeds

Wants: 2-bedroom in Liverpool

Reason: New job opportunity

Tenant C: Michael (Liverpool)

Current: 2-bedroom flat in Liverpool

Wants: 2-bedroom in Manchester

Reason: Closer to partner

Forms a Circle!

David needs to relocate from Manchester into the Leeds area currently served by Sarah's provider.
Sarah needs to relocate from Leeds into Liverpool where Michael lives.
Michael needs to relocate from Liverpool into Manchester, where David lives.
Housing providers can align these needs so everyone moves.

The Journey

Step 1-2

Requests Logged by Housing Teams

Housing officers for David, Sarah, and Michael each record the tenants' needs in BridgeConnect. They don't know about one another yet-the system will spot the link.

Step 3

Circular Chain Detected

The matching system discovers the circular pattern: A → B → C → A. It creates a 3-way exchange linking all three transfers together.

Step 4-5

Three Housing Associations Approve

Manchester HA approves David's participation. Leeds HA approves Sarah's participation. Liverpool HA approves Michael's participation. All approvals secured!

Step 6

Coordinated Move Date

Housing providers agree on a shared move date with the tenants. This requires careful coordination-everyone must move on the same day.

Step 7

The Big Move Day

David moves to Leeds, Sarah moves to Liverpool, Michael moves to Manchester. Housing providers oversee key exchanges so all three transfers complete simultaneously. The chain is complete!

Chain Coordination

In a 3-way chain, if any tenant backs out, the entire chain breaks and all three moves are cancelled. Careful coordination and commitment are essential.

Why Chains Matter

Without the chain, none of these moves would happen. There's no vacant property for David in Leeds, no vacant property for Sarah in Liverpool, and no vacant property for Michael in Manchester. But by forming a chain, all three can move! This is the power of mutual exchange.

Result

David is near his parents in Leeds. Sarah has her new job in Liverpool. Michael is with his partner in Manchester. Three families helped by one chain! Total time: 7 weeks.
